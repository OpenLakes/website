What data teams are up against

We focus on the business problems that keep modern platforms from scaling sustainably.

Runaway consumption bills

Enterprises continue to cite cloud cost optimization as their #1 priority according to the 2024 State of Cloud Spend survey.[1] Paying list price for every query, replica, and feature makes budgeting nearly impossible.

Regulatory pressure

A 2024 privacy forecast projects that 75% of the world’s population will have its personal data covered by modern regulations.[2] Handing telemetry to a third-party SaaS vendor often complicates compliance instead of simplifying it.

Disconnected cost accountability

The 2023 FinOps benchmark highlights “getting engineers to take action on cost insights” as the top challenge.[3] If teams cannot see infrastructure inputs, they cannot correct expensive patterns.

Siloed tooling choices

Every new SaaS tool introduces integration debt. Building a reference implementation once—and sharing the manifests openly—keeps stakeholders aligned on the same stack.

How OpenLakes helps

A purpose-built reference implementation that balances financial, compliance, and engineering priorities.

Transparent cost modeling

Every layer lists the images, storage classes, and resource requests it relies on. Finance teams can map infrastructure invoices directly to workloads instead of guessing at opaque consumption credits.

Control without rewrites

The deployer wires Iceberg, Trino, Spark, Airflow, Superset, Meltano, and OpenMetadata together with sane defaults so you avoid a multi-month integration program.

Audit-friendly operations

Because OpenLakes runs on your Kubernetes clusters, access, logging, and residency follow the same guardrails you already enforce elsewhere.

Documented playbooks

Each Helm chart, docker image, and example notebook is versioned under Apache 2.0. You can reproduce, extend, or certificate-review every change before it reaches production.
