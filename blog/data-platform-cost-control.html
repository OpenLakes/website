Bring Data Infrastructure Costs Back in Line

A practical look at how to taper cloud data warehouse bills without giving up the reliability teams expect.

Cloud data warehouses like Snowflake and Databricks have become essential infrastructure for modern enterprises. But they come with a hidden cost: at scale, these platforms can consume millions of dollars annually in compute, storage, and egress fees. OpenLakes offers a different path.

The Hidden Cost of Cloud Data Warehouses

Many teams don't realize the full cost of their data infrastructure until it's too late. Cloud data warehouse pricing models charge for:

  • Compute — Per-second or per-query charges that scale with usage
  • Storage — Monthly fees for every terabyte stored
  • Data egress — Charges for moving data out of the platform
  • Vendor features — Premium add-ons for governance, security, and compliance

At 10TB, a typical cloud data warehouse costs $50,000-$100,000 annually. At 100TB, costs balloon to $500,000-$1,000,000. Petabyte-scale deployments can exceed $5 million per year.

OpenLakes: Enterprise Capabilities at Infrastructure Cost

OpenLakes deploys a complete data lakehouse platform on your existing Kubernetes infrastructure.[1] Instead of paying cloud vendors for compute and storage, you pay only for the underlying infrastructure you already control.

Key insight: OpenLakes separates compute from storage. You can scale query engines independently of data storage, avoiding the "pay for everything together" model of traditional cloud data warehouses.

Deployment Flexibility

OpenLakes supports multiple deployment profiles based on your infrastructure:[9]

  • Compact (10 vCPU / 18GB RAM) — Single-node deployments for development and small workloads
  • Small (10 vCPU / 24-32GB RAM) — Single-node production with local storage
  • Full (24+ cores / 128GB+ RAM) — Multi-node clusters with distributed storage

Start small on a single node, then scale horizontally as data volumes grow. The same deployment script handles both scenarios.[10]

No Hidden Costs

OpenLakes is 100% open source under the Apache 2.0 license. There are no:

  • Per-query charges
  • Per-user fees
  • Storage tiering premiums
  • Enterprise feature gates
  • Egress fees (your data stays in your infrastructure)

A Simple Decision Framework

OpenLakes makes financial sense when:

  1. You have existing Kubernetes infrastructure or are willing to invest in it
  2. Your data volumes exceed 10TB (cost savings increase with scale)
  3. You have platform engineering capacity to operate the deployment
  4. You value long-term cost predictability over pay-as-you-go convenience
  5. Data sovereignty matters (regulatory, security, or strategic reasons)
